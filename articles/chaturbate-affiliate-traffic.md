# Chaturbate Affiliate Traffic Explained

Chaturbate runs one of the more broadcaster-friendly affiliate programs in the industry — and unlike some platforms, it's directly accessible to models, not just webmasters.

## How the Program Works

- **20% lifetime revshare** on token purchases made by users you refer, with no cap and no expiration on the relationship.
- **$1.00** paid for Tier 1 signups (people you refer directly), with smaller amounts for Tier 2 and Tier 3 referral chains.
- **$50 paid to broadcasters** for referring a new model who goes on to start earning — a direct incentive for models to bring other performers onto the platform.
- **5% rev-share for webmasters** who recruit other affiliates into the network.
- **White-label option** — affiliates can run their own branded cam site, fully powered by Chaturbate's models, streams, and payments behind the scenes.

## Why This Matters Beyond Extra Income

Referral signups don't just pay you directly — they also factor into [Chaturbate's ranking algorithm](chaturbate-algorithm). Bringing in new registered users through your own affiliate link, combined with solid views and tips, is one of the few concrete levers models can pull to move up the front-page ranking beyond content and schedule alone.

## Which Traffic Sources Actually Work

For affiliate-driven traffic specifically, the sources that produce the most revenue per hour invested are **SEO content, Reddit, and Twitter/X** — quality matters more than volume here, since referred users who actually spend are worth far more than a large batch of low-intent signups.

## Should You Run This Yourself?

Unlike Stripchat's affiliate system (which is built mainly for professional webmasters), Chaturbate's program is genuinely usable by an individual model:

1. Get your own affiliate link and use it whenever you share your room externally instead of a plain profile link.
2. Focus referral effort on channels where you already have some credibility (a niche blog, Reddit community, or engaged social following).
3. Track which channel actually produces referrals who spend, not just signups.
