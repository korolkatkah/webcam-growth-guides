# Chaturbate OBS Settings

Chaturbate doesn't require exotic settings — a safe, well-tested baseline outperforms chasing maximum resolution on a connection that can't support it.

## Recommended Starting Settings

- **Resolution:** start at **720p** rather than jumping straight to 1080p or 4K — Chaturbate's stream handling doesn't reward higher resolution if it costs you stability.
- **Bitrate:** start around **3,000 kbps** for 720p. General bitrate guidelines for reference: **2,500–4,000 kbps** for 720p, **4,500–6,000 kbps** as a ceiling for 1080p.
- **Audio bitrate:** at least **128 kbps** — viewers judge audio quality more harshly than most models expect.
- **Rate control:** CBR (constant bitrate) for the most consistent live-streaming quality.
- **Keyframe interval:** 2 seconds, a safe default for most platforms' transcoding.

## The Bandwidth Rule

Your combined audio + video bitrate must stay comfortably under your actual upload speed — not your advertised plan speed. Test your real upload, then keep your OBS output at roughly 70–80% of that tested number to leave headroom for fluctuation.

## Don't Overshoot Your Camera or Connection

Streaming above what your camera actually supports, or above what your connection can sustain, doesn't improve your stream — it just increases the odds of dropped frames and buffering, which actively hurts viewer retention (a core ranking signal — see [Chaturbate Algorithm](chaturbate-algorithm)).

## Before Every Session

1. Run an actual upload speed test rather than trusting your plan's advertised number.
2. Do a short local test recording to check exposure, framing, and audio before going live.
3. Watch OBS's dropped-frames counter in your first few minutes — persistent drops mean your bitrate is set too high for current conditions.

## Why This Matters Beyond Just Looking Good

Stream stability directly affects viewer retention, which is one of the most heavily weighted signals in Chaturbate's ranking. A technically solid, reliable stream is as much a growth tactic as it is a production detail — and it matters just as much if you're also running [external traffic promotion](https://ewohub.com/model-promotion), since a shaky stream is what makes newly arrived viewers leave.
