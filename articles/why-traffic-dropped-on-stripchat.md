# Why My Traffic Dropped on Stripchat

A sudden drop in viewers is rarely one single cause — it's usually one of a small set of recurring issues. Work through this list in order; it covers the most common causes roughly from most to least frequent.

## 1. Your Schedule Shifted

Even a small, unannounced change in your streaming hours can cost you your regulars and confuse the algorithm's expectations for your room. Check this first — it's the easiest to accidentally cause and the easiest to fix.

## 2. You're Outside Your Audience's Active Window

Traffic isn't flat across the day. If your hours drifted away from when your specific audience is actually online, viewership drops even if everything else stayed the same. See [Best Streaming Hours on Stripchat](best-streaming-hours-on-stripchat).

## 3. Your Tags Got More Competitive

Tag competition shifts over time as other models adopt the same tags. A tag that used to put you in a moderate-competition pool (roughly 500–2,000 rooms) can quietly become oversaturated.

## 4. Your Thumbnail or Preview Got Stale

If your live preview hasn't changed in a while, click-through can fade even with an unchanged show — viewers scrolling the model list are influenced heavily by that first-impression frame.

## 5. You're Still in (or Just Left) the New-Account Calibration Window

New rooms typically take 4–8 weeks for the platform's systems to fully calibrate. A drop right around that window is common and often self-corrects — it isn't always a sign something is wrong.

## 6. Engagement Dropped, Even If Viewer Count Didn't

Chat activity and tip frequency function as quality signals independent of raw viewer count. A quieter room — even with the same number of people watching — can rank lower. See [How StripScore Works](how-stripscore-works).

## 7. You Stopped Promoting Off-Platform

If part of your traffic came from social media, forums, or a personal page and that channel went quiet, the drop shows up on Stripchat even though nothing changed on the platform itself.
