# Chaturbate New Model Tag: What It Is and How to Use It

New broadcasters on Chaturbate get flagged with a "new" status that gives fresh rooms extra visibility while the algorithm is still learning your account. How you use that window shapes everything that follows.

## Why the New Model Tag Exists

Chaturbate's ranking algorithm relies heavily on historical signals — retention patterns, engagement history, returning followers — that a brand-new account simply doesn't have yet. The new model tag compensates for that gap by giving new rooms a visibility boost that isn't purely earned by track record yet, so new broadcasters get a fair shot at being seen against established performers.

## Making the Most of the Window

1. **Be available as much as realistically possible** during this early period — the extra visibility is temporary, and the audience you capture now can become your first regulars.
2. **Get your thumbnail and title right from day one.** Since click-through rate matters immediately, don't waste the boosted exposure on a weak first impression.
3. **Prioritize chat engagement over passive streaming.** The tag gets you seen; engagement converts that visibility into tips and returning viewers.
4. **Pick your hashtags deliberately before your first stream**, not after — see [Chaturbate Hashtags](chaturbate-hashtags).
5. **Set a schedule early and stick to it.** Consistency compounds, and starting the habit during your boosted window means it's already established once the tag fades.

## What Happens After the Tag Fades

Once the new-model boost ends, your position depends increasingly on the same fundamentals covered in [Chaturbate Algorithm](chaturbate-algorithm) — retention, engagement, and consistency, now measured against your own real track record instead of borrowed visibility. A drop in placement around this transition is normal and doesn't necessarily mean something is wrong; see [Why My Chaturbate Traffic Dropped](why-chaturbate-traffic-dropped) if it feels larger than expected.
