# How StripScore Works

StripScore is Stripchat's internal ranking mechanism — it's the reason two models with similar follower counts can end up in very different positions in the model list. Here's what actually feeds into it.

## The Core Signal: Tokens Per Hour

The single most heavily weighted input is how many tokens you earn per hour of broadcasting. Consistency matters more than peaks — a model who earns steadily every session outranks one who has one huge night followed by several quiet ones, even if their totals are similar.

## Profile Completeness

Having at least one photo album and one video on your profile produces a measurable lift. This is a one-time fix that costs nothing but time, and it's often skipped by newer models.

## Stream Quality

A stable connection and a working microphone are treated as baseline requirements, not nice-to-haves. Frequent drops, lag, or audio issues actively work against your position — not just your viewer experience.

## Content Variety

Rooms that offer varied, engaging shows rather than a static routine hold their score better over time. This overlaps with what keeps human viewers around, which is generally a good sign that the signal is measuring something real.

## Following Platform Guidelines

Accounts that stick to Stripchat's recommended practices tend to rank more favorably. This one is vague by design — platforms don't fully publish their guideline weighting — but it's consistently mentioned as a factor.

## How This Differs From the Broader Algorithm

StripScore is specifically about *your account's* internal ranking score. It sits alongside broader ranking mechanics like current viewer count, retention, and chat activity — covered in [Stripchat Algorithm](stripchat-algorithm) — which are more about how a given session performs in real time.
