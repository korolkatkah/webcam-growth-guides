# Stripchat Promo: How StripScore Works and How to Increase Viewers

Growing a room on Stripchat comes down to two things working together: the platform's internal ranking system (**StripScore**) and a deliberate **Stripchat promo** strategy that drives outside traffic to your room. This guide breaks down both, plus practical steps you can apply today to increase viewers.

## What Is Stripchat StripScore?

StripScore is Stripchat's internal ranking system. It decides where your room appears in the model list — and room position is one of the biggest drivers of how many people ever see your stream in the first place.

StripScore is calculated from several show- and profile-related factors, including:

- **Tokens earned per hour of broadcasting** — this is the single most heavily weighted signal. Consistent, steady earnings per hour matter more than a single lucky spike.
- **Profile completeness** — having at least one photo album and one video on your profile measurably helps your score.
- **Stream quality** — a stable internet connection and a working microphone are baseline requirements; drops and lag hurt your position.
- **Variety of shows** — offering diverse, engaging content rather than a static routine keeps your score healthier over time.
- **Following platform guidelines** — accounts that stick to Stripchat's recommended practices tend to rank more favorably.

In short: StripScore rewards consistency and profile quality, not just raw luck. Improving any one of these factors nudges your room higher in the list, which in turn increases your organic viewers.

## How to Increase Viewers on Stripchat

Once you understand StripScore, the next question is what actually moves the needle in a session. A few things consistently work:

**1. Upgrade your lighting first.** Lighting affects perceived stream quality more than almost any other single change — better lighting makes the room look more professional, which keeps viewers around longer and increases tips.

**2. Use interactive toys.** Rooms with interactive toy support report significantly higher tip volume compared to rooms without — often 2–4x — because they turn passive viewers into active participants.

**3. Structure your tip menu in three tiers.** A menu that mixes micro-actions (25–75 tokens), mid-tier items (100–300 tokens — typically the highest-volume tier), and premium actions (500–2,000+ tokens) gives viewers at every spending level a reason to tip. Display the menu in your bio, as an on-screen overlay, and repeat it via chatbot every 10–15 minutes.

**4. Run goals and games.** Group goals and simple games give the whole room a shared reason to participate, which keeps viewers in the room longer — and time-in-room is itself a positive signal that feeds back into your ranking.

**5. Keep transitions and production tight.** Quick scene changes, sound effects, and smooth transitions read as "professional," and professional-feeling streams consistently earn more.

**6. Diversify beyond live hours.** Fan clubs, subscription content, and clips let you earn (and stay visible) even when you're not broadcasting, and they help build the regular following that turns into private-show income.
