# Stripchat Algorithm: How Room Ranking Actually Works

Cam platform algorithms, Stripchat included, optimize for one core objective: maximizing viewer time spent on the platform. Everything the algorithm rewards traces back to that goal — which is why genuine engagement consistently outperforms vanity metrics.

## The Signals That Actually Matter

- **Current viewer count** — helps, but it's evaluated relative to your category and time of day, not as an absolute number.
- **Viewer retention** — long session durations and low bounce rates are a strong quality signal.
- **Follower-to-viewer ratio** — a high proportion of returning fans (vs. one-time visitors) indicates a loyal audience and strengthens ranking.
- **Chat activity** — active, ongoing conversation outperforms a silent room with the same viewer count.
- **Tip frequency** — many small tips signal sustained engagement better than one large, rare tip.
- **Streaming consistency** — regular, predictable schedules help the algorithm promote your room with confidence.
- **Session length** — sessions running past roughly 90 minutes show measurable ranking improvements.

## What Doesn't Work

High follower counts or profile views alone don't lift live rankings — only actual engagement during a session does. Platforms also detect artificially inflated engagement (bots, click farms) through behavioral analysis, so shortcuts here tend to backfire rather than help.

## StripScore vs. the Broader Algorithm

StripScore is the specific internal score tied to your account (see [How StripScore Works](how-stripscore-works)). The signals above are the broader, session-level mechanics that determine how a given broadcast performs in real time — the two work together rather than being separate systems.

## Turning This Into a Growth Plan

1. Build sessions that reward retention — pacing, goals, and games that keep people watching past the first few minutes.
2. Prioritize chat engagement over passive viewing — ask questions, react to comments, keep the room alive.
3. Structure tipping around frequent small actions rather than rare big ones.
4. Keep a consistent schedule the algorithm (and your regulars) can learn.
5. Push sessions past the 90-minute mark where your energy and content allow it.
